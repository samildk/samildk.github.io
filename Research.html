Persistent Homology of the Cosmic Web

Master Thesis | Prof. S. M. S. Movahed

View on GitHub

Study of large scale cosmology can provide information about the initial conditions and evolution of the universe. In the linear regime, the power spectrum can generally establish a meaningful relationship between observation and theory, and thus provide constraints on the free parameters of the model. However, with the entry into the non-linear regime and small scales k ≳ 0.1 Mpc h-1, due to non-linear effects, numerous degeneracies are created for parameters such as σ8, ns, and Ωm. On the other hand, the power spectrum in such regimes is mostly insensitive and the use of alternative tools is preferred.

In this research, we investigate the evolution of the cosmic web using the persistent homology approach. We follow the dependency of the Betti curves, persistence diagrams, and persistence entropy of cosmic structures to high redshifts. The zeroth, first, and second homology groups represent clusters, filaments, and voids, respectively. The persistence of homology groups by performing upper-level filtration at different redshifts can show the footprint of the hierarchical evolution of the cosmic web.

In this research, we use the Quijote and Concept N-body simulations to investigate the dark matter density contrast. For this purpose, we use simulations based on the LambdaCDM model with minor changes in parameters such as (σ8, ω) at different redshifts, which are in a box of size L ~ 1 Gpc h-1 and contain N = (512)3 particles.

Persistence entropy diagrams are a new measure in which we can show that at different redshifts, the ratio of prior results has a lower percentage error. Our results show that β0 and β2 are strongly affected by the change in redshift, while β1 receives the least effect. Persistence entropy shows that the evolution of filaments is more dependent on redshift than voids and clusters. On the other hand, voids are more sensitive to constrain σ8 and clusters are more sensitive to constrain ωm, and this sensitivity is also preserved in z ≳ 0.

Dynamical Evolution of the AGN Central SMBHs Using Kramers-Moyal Expansion

Reseach Assitant Project | Dr. Javad Taghizadeh Firuzjaee

One of the main problems concerning quasars involves their central supermassive black holes (SMBHs). The explanation for the formation of these SMBHs remains unknown, and several ideas have been presented to explain them. However, the community has yet to accept a comprehensive explanation. Only one thing is certain: these black holes were not formed by the collapse of giant stars or the accumulation of matter surrounding them.

Furthermore, another crucial topic concerns the mass distribution of these black holes over time. In our previous work, we approximated the mass of quasars central supermassive black holes over time using QusarNET datasets and Long Short Term Memory (LSTM). This work is based on data reported from redshifts 3 to 7.1. We utilized this model to reconstruct SMBHs data. As a result, we used the histogram approach to determine the constants of the Kramers-Moyal expansion. This method is a more efficient strategy for determining the dynamic evolution of SMBHs.

Philosophy Of Newtonian Mechanics To Special Relativity

Bachelor Thesis | Dr. Hossein Shojaee

A conceptual exploration of how philosophical interpretations of space, time, and causality evolved from Newtonian mechanics to Einsteinian special relativity, and how these shifts influenced modern views of determinism, locality, and the nature of physical laws.
