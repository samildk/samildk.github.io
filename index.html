SAMIRA ASLANI

Cosmologist and Data Scientist

About Me

I am a cosmology researcher and a working data scientist. My M.Sc. at Shahid Beheshti University focused on reading the cosmic web with persistent homology, including Betti curves, persistence diagrams, and a persistent entropy metric to link topological structure with cosmological signals. I have also worked on a quasar and SMBH growth study using Kramers-Moyal expansion. In industry, I design and ship pragmatic vision pipelines. Recent work includes real-time video ad detection at scale and deterministic image clustering, using Python, PyTorch, OpenCV, FFmpeg, SQL/SQLAlchemy, and PCA, with reproducible code and measurable outcomes. I also teach Python and data analysis and have served as a TA for numerical simulation and quantum mechanics.

Research focus

Large-scale structure, galaxy clustering, N-body simulations, and topological data analysis in cosmology; SMBHs, 21 cm cosmology, and the epoch of reionization; plus statistical physics and stochastic processes when they sharpen the toolkit.

What I build

End-to-end data and computer-vision systems that move from prototype to production: data modeling, feature engineering, training and evaluation, and deployment on real data streams. Tooling: Python scientific stack, PyTorch, OpenCV, SQL, Linux, Git.

Teaching and outreach

Tutor and instructor for Python, data analysis, and scientific computing; TA for fundamentals of numerical simulation; talks and defenses on persistent homology of the cosmic web.

Around the lab

h3> I am also a photographer with documented work and an award in student theater, and I have a long history of physics outreach and astronomy activities. I play guitar, sing, run, practice yoga, and read widely in literature, music, psychology, mindfulness, travel, and philosophy.

How I work

Direct, curious, and results-driven. I care about clear problem statements, auditable pipelines, and code that another person can actually run. My long-term goal is simple: do serious cosmology and build solid, usable data systems.

The honest closer

I am multipotential and highly creative. I do not claim depth in every domain outside cosmology and programming, but I bring strong pattern recognition, fast learning, and careful taste across them. That range is an asset, not a distraction. It feeds original ideas and better problem solving, and I use it in service of the work that matters most to me.
